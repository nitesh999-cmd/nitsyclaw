# The Real Estate Lead Follow-Up Sequence Template That Actually Gets Replies

## Why Most Real Estate Follow-Up Fails

Before the template, it helps to understand why follow-up breaks down even when agents intend to do it.

**Problem 1: No system, only intentions.** "I'll follow up next week" is not a system. It is a mental note that competes with 50 other mental notes. Without a written sequence and a trigger to send each message, follow-up is inconsistent by default.

**Problem 2: The follow-up stops when it gets uncomfortable.** Most agents feel awkward sending a fourth or fifth message to someone who hasn't replied. But silence from a lead is not rejection — it is usually busyness. A well-written message after ten days of silence regularly gets a "sorry, been busy — yes I'm still looking" reply.

**Problem 3: The messages are too salesy.** "Just checking in to see if you're still interested!" is easy to ignore. A message that offers something specific — a relevant listing, a useful piece of area information, an easy way out — gets opened because it looks useful.

**Problem 4: No automation.** If sending the day-8 message requires a human to remember to do it, it will not be sent reliably. The only follow-up system that survives a busy month is one that runs itself.

## The 7-Touch / 14-Day Real Estate Follow-Up Sequence

This cadence is designed for leads who have made initial contact but are not yet ready to book an appointment — typically "still exploring" or "3–6 months out" responses. It runs over 14 days, stops the moment the lead replies (any reply), and includes a clear opt-out in the final message.

**Rules before you copy this:**
- Every sequence must stop immediately on any inbound reply. Never continue automated messages when a human conversation is underway.
- Include opt-out language (STOP to opt out) in at least the first and last message.
- Respect quiet hours — no sends before 8am or after 9pm local time.
- All bracketed fields `[like this]` are personalised per lead. The AI or assistant fills them from the lead record.
- **Compliance note:** Automated SMS to consumers is regulated (TCPA in the US, similar frameworks elsewhere). Ensure you have appropriate consent from the lead source before running automated texts. See your legal counsel or SMS provider for the rules in your jurisdiction — this is a business-critical gate, not optional.

### Touch 1 — Day 0: Instant first response (within 2 minutes of lead submission)

**Purpose:** Acknowledge the lead while they are still in the moment. Begin the qualification conversation.

> "Hi [First Name], thanks for reaching out about [area/property]! I'm [Agency Name]'s assistant — I can get you connected to the right agent quickly. Mind if I ask a couple of quick questions so they can actually help? 😊 Reply STOP anytime to opt out."

This is the most important message in the sequence. Everything else is follow-up — this is the opening. See our article on [how to respond to real estate leads faster] for the full speed-to-lead system.

---

### Touch 2 — Day 1: Gentle bump

**Purpose:** Re-engage leads who didn't reply to the opener. Keep it short and low-pressure.

> "Hi [First Name] — still happy to help you look around [area] whenever you're ready. No rush at all. Just reply here if you'd like a hand."

---

### Touch 3 — Day 3: Value, not ask

**Purpose:** Give the lead a reason to reply that isn't just "are you still interested." Offer something specific.

> "A couple of [area] listings just came up that might be worth a look — want me to send them over? Happy to filter by what matters to you."

*Variant for seller leads:*
> "We just had a property sell in [suburb] — happy to share what it went for and what that means for yours. Want the numbers?"

---

### Touch 4 — Day 5: Soft question

**Purpose:** Invite the lead to tell you what has changed. Often surfaces "actually, we've delayed" or "yes, let's talk" — both useful.

> "Quick one — are you still actively looking, or has the timing shifted a bit? Either's totally fine, just want to make sure I'm not clogging up your messages if things have changed."

---

### Touch 5 — Day 8: Helpful resource

**Purpose:** Offer something genuinely useful — no ask attached. Builds goodwill and keeps the door open.

> "No pressure at all from our end. If it's helpful, [Agent Name] put together a short [buying/selling] guide for [area] — covers what to expect on timelines, pricing, and what questions to ask. Want me to send it over?"

*If you don't have a guide yet, a link to a useful public resource (a relevant market report, a reputable first-home-buyer checklist) works fine.*

---

### Touch 6 — Day 11: Re-engage with an easy out

**Purpose:** Check whether a monthly check-in would suit better than this pace. This message often gets a reply because it offers control back to the lead.

> "Still here whenever the timing works for you, [First Name]. Would it be easier if I checked back in a month instead of now? Happy to do whatever suits."

---

### Touch 7 — Day 14: Polite close

**Purpose:** Close the active sequence with a door left open. This message often gets replies from leads who felt guilty about not responding.

> "I'll leave you to it from here — don't want to be a pest. 😊 Whenever [area] is back on the radar, just reply to this thread and we'll pick it straight back up. Reply STOP if you'd rather we didn't check in. Hope the [buying/selling] journey goes well!"

## Adapting the Sequence for Different Lead Types

| Lead type | Key adaptation |
|---|---|
| Buyer — active (< 3 months) | Shorten to 5 touches over 7 days; move booking link to Touch 2 |
| Buyer — exploring (> 3 months) | Use the full 14-day cadence above; focus on value not urgency |
| Seller — thinking about listing | Swap property listings for market data and comparable sales |
| Seller — ready now | Skip nurture entirely, go straight to valuation booking |
| Rental / not a fit | One message confirming you can't help, then suppress |
| No reply to all 7 touches | Move to a "long sleep" — one check-in at 30 days, then retire the lead |

## The 30-Day Reactivation (for leads who go completely dark)

If a lead reaches Touch 7 with no response, do not delete them. Move them to a 30-day reactivation:

> "Hi [First Name] — [Agent Name] here. It's been a month since you enquired about [area]. Just wanted to check — are you still on the lookout, or has the plan changed? No worries either way, just don't want you to miss something good if we can help."

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How many follow-up messages is too many?

Seven touches over 14 days, with a polite close at the end, is the upper limit for most leads who have not replied at all. Beyond that, you are more likely to annoy than to convert. The exception is leads who have had any engagement (even just one reply) — those warrant more patient, conversational follow-up at a slower pace.

### Should follow-up be by text, email, or call?

Text (SMS or WhatsApp) consistently outperforms email for response rates on lead follow-up — people read texts faster and respond more readily. Phone calls can work for highly qualified leads but often feel intrusive for cold contacts. The most effective approach is text-first, with a phone call offered once the lead has shown they are ready.

### What if a lead says "not interested" on touch 2?

Honour it immediately and permanently. Send one final message: "No problem at all — I've taken you off our list. Good luck with whatever you decide, and feel free to reach out if anything changes." Then suppress them. Chasing a lead who has said no is the fastest way to damage your brand.

### Does this sequence comply with SMS regulations?

The cadence above is designed around best practices (clear opt-out, no quiet-hours sends, identified sender), but compliance depends on your jurisdiction, lead source, and SMS provider setup. In the US, A2P 10DLC registration and TCPA consent requirements apply. Consult a qualified source for your specific situation before running automated texts.

### What tools do I need to run this automatically?

At minimum: an SMS-capable CRM or a no-code tool (Make, Zapier, ActiveCampaign) connected to your lead source. Alternatively, a done-for-you service handles the entire technical layer for you.
