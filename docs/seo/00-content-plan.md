## 1. Target Keyword Themes

The goal is to own the bottom of the funnel for a real estate broker or team lead who is already
feeling the pain of lost leads. These readers have the problem, have budget awareness, and are
searching for a solution — not for definitions.

### Theme A — Speed to Lead
Pain: the broker knows slow response kills conversions and wants to fix it.
Examples:
- "how to respond to real estate leads faster"
- "speed to lead real estate"
- "real estate lead response time best practices"
- "how fast should you respond to Zillow leads"
- "real estate lead response automation"

### Theme B — Follow-Up Sequences
Pain: the broker's follow-up is inconsistent or dies after two attempts.
Examples:
- "real estate lead follow up sequence template"
- "real estate follow up texts templates"
- "how many times to follow up with a real estate lead"
- "real estate lead nurture sequence"
- "7-touch follow up real estate"

### Theme C — Why Leads Are Lost / Wasted
Pain: the broker has a sinking feeling they're burning money on leads that go nowhere.
Examples:
- "why real estate agents lose leads"
- "why real estate leads don't convert"
- "real estate lead conversion problems"
- "wasted real estate leads"
- "real estate agents not following up"

### Theme D — Automated Qualification
Pain: the broker is interested in automating the front-end qualifying work.
Examples:
- "how to qualify real estate leads automatically"
- "real estate lead qualification questions"
- "automated real estate lead qualification"
- "AI real estate lead qualification"
- "best way to qualify real estate leads"

### Theme E — Local / City Variants (build after case study exists)
Pain: same as above, but with local search intent — "near me" buyers with higher commercial intent.
Examples:
- "[city] real estate lead automation"
- "[city] real estate lead follow up service"
- "real estate lead response service [state]"

## 3. On-Page SEO Checklist (per article)

Use this before hitting publish on every article.

### Title Tag
- [ ] Includes the primary keyword (ideally near the front)
- [ ] 50–60 characters (does not truncate in SERPs)
- [ ] Makes a promise or implies a benefit, not just a label
- [ ] Example pattern: "[Benefit/Outcome]: [keyword]" or "[Keyword] — [What They Get]"

### Meta Description
- [ ] 140–155 characters
- [ ] Restates the pain + the article's answer
- [ ] Includes a soft CTA ("See how", "Get the template", "Learn the system")
- [ ] Does not duplicate the title word-for-word

### H1
- [ ] One H1 per page, matches the title tag in intent (does not have to be identical)
- [ ] Reads naturally — not keyword-stuffed

### H2 / H3 Structure
- [ ] H2s break the article into logical sections (not just decorative)
- [ ] At least one H2 targets a secondary or related keyword phrase naturally
- [ ] FAQ section uses H2 "Frequently Asked Questions" + H3 per question (enables FAQ rich results)
- [ ] No heading skips (H1 → H2 → H3, not H1 → H3)

### Body Content
- [ ] Primary keyword appears in the first 100 words
- [ ] Natural keyword usage throughout — no keyword stuffing
- [ ] At least one copy-paste template, checklist, or table (gives the article "asset" value)
- [ ] Specific, credible, actionable — not generic "communication is important" filler
- [ ] Unverified statistics are labelled as such before publishing (see top of this doc)
- [ ] Target word count: 1,000–1,500 words for MOFU articles; 800–1,200 for BOFU; 1,500–2,000 for
  pillar/comparison pieces

### Internal Linking
- [ ] Link to at least 2 other articles in the cluster (use descriptive anchor text, not "click here")
- [ ] Accept inbound links from related articles in the cluster
- [ ] At least one link to the main service/demo page

### CTA Placement
- [ ] Soft CTA in or just below the intro (before they bounce)
- [ ] Hard CTA at the bottom (clear offer + action)
- [ ] Optional mid-article CTA after the main value section (for long articles)
- [ ] CTA copy is specific: "Book a 20-minute demo" beats "Contact us"

### Schema / FAQ
- [ ] FAQ section present with at least 3–4 genuine questions
- [ ] Consider adding FAQ schema markup (JSON-LD) for FAQ rich results in SERPs
- [ ] For how-to content, consider HowTo schema

### Images / Media
- [ ] Alt text on all images (descriptive, includes keyword where natural)
- [ ] File names are descriptive (not IMG_1234.jpg)
- [ ] Images are compressed (under 150KB where possible)

### Technical
- [ ] URL slug is short, keyword-focused, lowercase, hyphenated
- [ ] Canonical tag is set
- [ ] Page loads fast on mobile (check before publishing)
- [ ] No broken links

## 4. CTA Strategy

Every article should funnel toward one of two outcomes:
1. **Book a demo call** — for readers who are research-done and want to talk.
2. **Text the demo number** — for readers who want to see the system work in real time.

Use `{demo_link}` and `{demo_number}` as placeholders until the operator confirms live values.
Do not invent URLs or phone numbers in published content.

The closing CTA in every article should:
- Acknowledge the reader's pain one more time (echo the intro pain)
- Make the offer concrete and low-risk ("14-day pilot, refundable if we don't beat your current
  response time")
- Remove the obvious objection ("you don't need to learn new software")
- Give one clear next step

## 5. Keyword Validation Reminder

Before committing to any article in this calendar:
1. Run the target keyword through Ahrefs or Semrush.
2. Check KD (Keyword Difficulty) — for a new domain, prioritise KD < 20 to start.
3. Check MSV (Monthly Search Volume) — even 50–200 searches/month is viable for bottom-funnel.
4. Check SERP intent — make sure the articles ranking now match your intended content type.
5. Check for featured snippet / PAA (People Also Ask) opportunities — these are quick wins.
